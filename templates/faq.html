Frequently Asked Questions

1. Coin Possession

Coin ownership is divided into two types of entities:

  • Participants: Limited to X individuals who can be senders and receivers.
  • Central Bank: Limited to 1 entity, acting as a sender and receiver.

2. Transparent Coin Ownership

All participants are aware of each other's coin holdings.

3. Total Coin Supply Visibility

Everyone knows the total number of coins in circulation.

4. Transaction Structure

Each transaction includes the following details:

  • Unique identifier
  • Timestamp
  • Sender
  • Receiver
  • Transaction subject

5. Immutable Transactions

Transactions cannot be modified unless both the sender and receiver agree on the change.

6. Transparency in Transactions

All transactions are visible to all participants.

7. Initial Coin Allocation

Upon entering the system, participants receive 100 coins.

8. Exiting the System

When exiting the system, coins are returned to the central bank.

9. Coin Redistribution

The central bank reallocates coins to participants based on their weekly transaction activity.
